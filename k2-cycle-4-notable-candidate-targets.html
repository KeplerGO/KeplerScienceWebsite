K2 Cycle 4: Notable candidate targets

News article posted on 03 Jan 2016 by Geert Barentsen

Proposals for targets to be observed by K2 in Campaigns 11, 12, and 13 are currently being solicited, with Step 1 due on February 5, 2016.

The fields of these campaigns will cover roughly 100 square degrees at a time near the constellations of Ophiucus (C11), Aquarius (C12), and Taurus (C13).

K2 is a community-led mission. All the targets that it observes are proposed and peer-reviewed by members of the community through the Guest Observer program.

To help the community select and propose the most interesting targets, we have created a catalog that lists all the 240,000 objects which are known to SIMBAD and fall inside the fields of view of future K2 Campaigns. The catalogue details object IDs, spectral types, object classifications, citation counts, photometry, and more. It can be downloaded from our GitHub repository.

In brief, the catalog reveals Campaigns 11 through 13 contain a wide range of known objects, including (but not limited to):

  • 2040 M-type stars;
  • 1270 RR Lyr Variables;
  • 1219 Quasars;
  • 727 High proper-motion stars
  • 280 Mira Variables;
  • 252 Young Stellar Objects;
  • 167 Active Galactic Nuclei;
  • 152 White Dwarfs (3 pulsating);
  • 133 Herbig Haro Objects;
  • 71 T Tauri Stars;
  • 57 Brown Dwarfs;
  • 29 Blue Stragglers;
  • 18 O-type stars;
  • 16 Cataclysmic Variables;
  • 11 Symbiotic Stars;
  • 4 Wolf Rayet stars;
  • 1 FU Orionis variable.

Notable objects in the field of view

Campaign 11 (Ophiucus; near the Galactic Center)

  • Saturn and its moons;
  • Messier 9: a 12 Gyr-old globular cluster, also known as NGC 6333;
  • BF Oph: a bright classical Cepheid;
  • Kepler's Supernova Remnant;
  • V2116 Oph: a symbiotic star, V=18;
  • Terzan 5: a global cluster in the Galactic Bulge;
  • Asteroid Ixion: a large dwarf planet;
  • Asteroid Sylvia: an asteroid with two moons;
  • Mars Trojan 311999.

Campaign 12 (Aquarius; South Galactic Cap)

  • Mars
  • HIP 116454: a known exoplanet host star;
  • Comet Chiron: a comet thought to have ring system;
  • Main Belt Comet 118401 LINEAR;
  • Asteroid Itokawa: visited by the Hayabuse spacecraft in 2005;

Campaign 13 (Taurus; near the Galactic anti-center)

  • Aldebaran: a famous K-type giant, V=0;
  • Asteroid Annefrank: visited by the Stardust spacecraft in 2002;
  • The Taurus Star-Forming Region, includes:
    • HL Tau: protoplanetary disk famously imaged by ALMA, V=15;
    • AA Tau: contains an unconfirmed transiting 20 MJup companion, V=12;
    • V1213 Tau: associated with the pretty Herbig Haro outflow HH 30;
    • LkCa15: thought to show an actively-forming exoplanet in direct imaging;
    • XZ Tau: binary young system which showed a superflare in 2000;
    • HBC 393: FUOr, i.e. extremely variable abrupt mass accretion events.
  • The Hyades open cluster, includes:
    • eps Tau: a bright member of the Hyades with a known RV planet, V=4;
  • NGC 1647, 1746, 1750, 1802, 1807: open clusters, less well-studied;
  • NGC 1817: 1 Gyr open cluster, ~2 kpc distance.
